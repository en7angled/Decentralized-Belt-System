# Frequently Asked Questions

## General

### What is BJJ Belts?

BJJ Belts is a decentralized application that provides a unified, transparent, and verifiable way to track belt credentials, lineage, achievements, and memberships in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ). Every record is enforced by smart contracts on the Cardano blockchain, making it tamper-proof and publicly verifiable.

### Why does BJJ need this?

Today there is no unified system to verify a practitioner's belt level or training history. Belt credentials can be hard to confirm — especially when practitioners move between academies, travel, or train across different regions. BJJ Belts solves this by creating a single, permanent record that anyone can verify.

### Who is BJJ Belts for?

- **Practitioners** who want an immutable record of their belt progression, achievements, and academy affiliations.
- **Instructors and Masters** who want a trustworthy way to issue and track promotions.
- **Academies, federations, and event organizers** who want to manage memberships and award achievements with full transparency.

### Is BJJ Belts free to use?

Creating a profile and browsing the BJJ network is open to everyone. Certain on-chain actions — such as belt promotions, memberships, and achievements — involve small transaction fees that go toward sustaining the network and its community treasury.

## Profiles & Belts

### How do I get started?

Connect a Cardano wallet (such as Eternl or Lace), create your practitioner profile, and you'll start as a White Belt — just like on the mat. From there, your instructor can promote you and your belt history will be recorded permanently.

### Can I create a profile for my academy or organization?

Yes. Organizations — academies, federations, event organizers — can create their own profiles to manage memberships and award achievements to their members.

### Can I update my profile after creating it?

Yes. You can update your profile details at any time — for example, if you switch academies or want to add new information.

### Can I delete my profile?

No. Profiles are permanent by design. Your belt history is a record of fact that preserves your lineage and the integrity of the BJJ network. Think of it like a diploma — once earned, it exists forever.

## Belt Promotions

### How do belt promotions work?

Promotions are a two-step process: your instructor initiates the promotion, and you accept it. Once both steps are complete, the new belt credential is recorded on-chain and becomes part of your permanent history.

### Who can promote me?

Only practitioners with the appropriate belt level can promote others, following standard BJJ promotion rules:
- **Anyone** can start as a White Belt.
- **Black Belts** can promote practitioners from Blue Belt up to Brown Belt.
- Promotion eligibility also considers time spent at your current belt level and age.

### Can someone fake a promotion?

No. Every promotion is validated by smart contracts that enforce the BJJ promotion rules. A promotion can only happen if the promoting instructor holds the required belt level and all protocol conditions are met.

### Can I see my full belt history?

Yes. Your profile displays your complete promotion history — every belt you've earned, when you earned it, and who promoted you.

## Lineage

### What is lineage?

Lineage is the direct chain of instructor–student relationships formed by belt promotions. Your lineage traces upward through the instructors who promoted you (and who promoted them), and downward through the students you've promoted (and their students in turn).

### Can I explore someone else's lineage?

Yes. Lineage is public and verifiable. You can explore any practitioner's lineage tree directly from their profile.

## Memberships & Achievements

### What are memberships?

Memberships represent your affiliation with an organization — an academy, federation, or other BJJ entity. Organizations can grant memberships, and either party can end them. Your membership history is permanently recorded.

### What are achievements?

Achievements are recognitions awarded by organizations or instructors — competition medals, seminar attendance, diplomas, and more. Once accepted, they become part of your permanent on-chain record.

### How do I receive a membership or achievement?

An organization or instructor initiates the membership or achievement, and you accept it through the app. Both steps are required for the record to be finalized.

## Wallet & Blockchain

### Do I need a Cardano wallet?

Yes. You'll need a browser-based Cardano wallet (such as Eternl or Lace) to sign transactions and interact with the BJJ network. Your wallet is how the system verifies your identity.

### Do I need to buy ADA (Cardano's currency)?

Yes, a small amount of ADA is needed to cover transaction fees on the Cardano network. These fees are minimal.

### Do I need to understand blockchain to use BJJ Belts?

Not at all. BJJ Belts is designed to feel like any other web application. The blockchain works behind the scenes to ensure your records are permanent, tamper-proof, and verifiable — you don't need to think about it.

### Is my personal information stored on the blockchain?

Your profile details and belt history are stored on-chain. Only information you choose to include in your profile is recorded. You control what goes into your profile.

## Trust & Security

### How can I trust that records are authentic?

Every action — promotions, achievements, memberships — is enforced by smart contracts that validate the rules before anything is recorded. No one can bypass these rules, not even the system administrators.

### What happens if someone claims a belt they didn't earn?

They can't. The smart contracts enforce promotion rules: the right instructor must initiate the promotion, and the practitioner must accept it. There is no way to self-promote or forge a belt credential.

### Who controls the system?

During the current phase, a Genesis Authority oversees protocol parameters. The long-term vision is to transition governance to a DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization), where the BJJ community itself — with voting power weighted by belt level — will make decisions about the network's future.

## Getting Involved

### I'm a Master / Black Belt. How do I get started?

During the Genesis Phase, recognized Masters receive personalized vouchers that allow them to claim their belt credentials on-chain. If you believe you should have received a voucher, please reach out to us.

### I run an academy. How can my organization join?

Any organization can create a profile and start managing memberships and achievements right away. Connect your wallet and set up your organization profile through the app.

### Where can I learn more or get in touch?

Visit our website or reach out through our community channels. BJJ Belts is a Project Catalyst-funded initiative (Proposal #1300081) building open-source tools for the global BJJ community.
